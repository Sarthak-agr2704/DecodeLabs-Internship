Frequency Pattern Preservation

MEDIUM RISK

Tiny Key Space (5-Bit Entropy)

With only 25 keys, a computer can check all decryption combinations in microseconds. This is vulnerable to brute-force attacks.

🔒 Cryptographic Evolution

To fix these issues, modern algorithms (like AES-256) introduce:

  • Confusion: Making the relationship between the key and the ciphertext as complex as possible.
  • Diffusion: Spreading the influence of individual plaintext characters over the entire ciphertext payload.
  • Large Key Space: 256-bit keys yield $1.15 \times 10^{77}$ combinations, resisting brute-force.
